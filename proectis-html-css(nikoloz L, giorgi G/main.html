The National Basketball Association (NBA) is the premier professional basketball league in the world. It was founded on June 6, 1946, in New York City as the Basketball Association of America (BAA). In 1949, the BAA merged with a rival league called the National Basketball League (NBL), and the organization was renamed the NBA. In its early years, the NBA struggled to gain popularity, but it grew rapidly in the 1950s and 1960s thanks to legendary players like Bill Russell and Wilt Chamberlain. The league became even more popular in the 1980s, driven by the rivalry between Magic Johnson and Larry Bird. In the 1990s, the NBA reached global fame largely due to Michael Jordan, who is widely considered one of the greatest basketball players of all time. Today, the league features 30 teams and includes international stars like LeBron James and Giannis Antetokounmpo. The NBA is known not only for its high level of competition but also for its cultural influence, shaping fashion, music, and global sports entertainment. It continues to grow worldwide, with millions of fans watching games across different countries.
